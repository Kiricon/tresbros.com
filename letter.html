Aloha to Family & Friends World-Wide:

     Gazing aside the roadway one can view the golden shades of the old sugar field swaying with the tropical breeze the Trade Winds, one of Kauai's gracious blessings.  Our time with each family member (We regard all friends as family)  is unique but whether from Arkansas, Ohio, Florida, Switzerland, Australia or New Zealand, the tranquility and beauty of the Garden Island is universal.....Gorgeous!  On any given day, we travel up the eastside, Gold Coast of the island.  Sharing the history and legends of the islands is a specialty of ours whether speaking to Auntie Em from Kansas or educating our visitors from outside the U.S.
"We treat our clients like family"
The old plantation town of Anahola maintains the largest population of Hawaiians, a proud culture with a magnificent heritage.  Passing the "Head of King Kong," the cool breeze is refreshing to the touch and you drive through a virtual paradise of tropical splendor.  The "Hanalei Look-Out," the mythical town of Hanalei, a famous dragon's origin lying aside the majestic blue shades of the glorious bay.  Across the bay perched high atop a volcanic bluff stands the luxurious Princeville Resort, famed location of the legendary "South Pacific."  The view of "Balihai" is a feast for the eyes no matter where you may call home.  Kauai is truly your own special island.  Our ability to share not only the overwhelming beauty of the Garden Island but to express the deep spiritual  grace of the "Aloha way of life" gives us pride in our work. When you are with us you also are a "local."
"Eduardo is that special friend of yours living on Kauai"
Balihai from Tunnels Beach
The coastal route past Hanalei Bay offers an awesome view.  Hollywood was so impressed with the area that they chose to film "South Pacific" here.  Lumahai Beach, where Mitzty Gainer "washed that man right out of her hair" in 1954 looks exactly the same today, radiant.  Between January and May above the beach is a great location to view the Humpback Whales as they return from Alaska with their newborn calves.  Next stop on our trail is tranquil Tunnels Beach and the five million year old Dry Cave.  The beach park is a favorite with campers, locals and visitors alike.
